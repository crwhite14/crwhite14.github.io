About

I am a research scientist at Meta, where I work on evaluating generative AI models. Previously, I was Head of Research at Abacus.AI. I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with a Ph.D. in computer science, advised by Nina Balcan and supported by the NDSEG Fellowship. I received my undergraduate degree from Amherst College.

For more, see my CV (last updated Dec. 2024).

My work spans topics in large language models, debiasing and explaining neural networks, tabular data, and automated machine learning, from both a theoretical and empirical lens. Much of my work involves designing tools for better evaluation of machine learning techniques and designing more efficient optimization methods.
